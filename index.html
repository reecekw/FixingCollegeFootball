Fixing College Football












Ways to fix the current College Football system

What is wrong with the current system?

The current "super-conference" system allows severe schedule disparity. Conferences are so big that teams don't end up playing each other. This 2024 season, Clemson, Miami, & SMU finished as the top 3 in the ACC. A problem the ACC nearly ran into is that those three teams did not play each other. If all three teams finished with the same record, the ACC would've had to use weird tiebreakers.

The “super-conferences” also force teams to travel cross country for in-conference games. In the ACC, the University of California-Berkeley traveled to Florida, Pennsylvania, North Carolina, & Texas for conference games. In the BIGTEN, the University of Oregon traveled to Indiana, Michigan, and Wisconsin. With these “super-conferences,” we lose some rivalries while these conferences try to create new ones between universities over a thousand miles apart.

What should change?

  1. Make the conferences smaller. No conference should have more than 10 teams.
  2. Divide these teams geographically. Oregon vs Oregon State means a lot more than Oregon vs Wisconsin.

But how do we keep these conference comissioners and those on the playoff committee happy? Money. How do we maximize revenue? The biggest programs playing each other while leaving room for other schools to grow. I propose a "Gold" division of FBS where 64 college football are divided into 8 difference conferences.

In August 2021, Zach Miller ranked the top 84 football programs based on 5 metrics: Home attendance, market share/size, valuation, social media following, & home attendance. This list can be found here. Although some of you may think the list is outdated, the top 64 of the 84 teams listed have been selected to create 8 conferences of 8 teams.
